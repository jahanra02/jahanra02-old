## Abstract

Accurate prediction of student academic performance is essential for enabling early intervention, personalized learning, and improving educational outcomes. This study investigates the use of the XGBoost algorithm for predicting student performance using behavioral and academic data. To address the lack of a unified evaluation framework in existing research, the study conceptually replicates and compares six state-of-the-art XGBoost-based approaches on a common student performance dataset. An enhanced modeling pipeline is proposed by integrating target distribution balancing through discretization for regression, Optuna-based hyperparameter optimization, and threshold-based feature selection. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed framework outperforms the adapted baseline methods, achieving an Adjusted R² score of **0.9685** and the lowest **RMSE of 3.6764**, highlighting its effectiveness for educational data analytics and student performance prediction.
