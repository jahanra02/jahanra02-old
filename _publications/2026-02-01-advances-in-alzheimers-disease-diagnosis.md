## Abstract

Alzheimer's disease (AD) is one of the leading causes of dementia worldwide, making early and accurate diagnosis essential for effective patient care. This review examines recent advances in machine learning (ML) and deep learning (DL) approaches for AD diagnosis. It discusses widely used public datasets, including ADNI, OASIS, and AIBL, along with data preprocessing techniques that improve model performance. The paper reviews state-of-the-art ML and DL methods, compares their effectiveness, and highlights current challenges and future research opportunities. The findings demonstrate the growing potential of artificial intelligence to support clinicians in achieving more accurate and timely Alzheimer's disease diagnosis.
